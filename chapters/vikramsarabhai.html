about vikram sarabhai

Dr. Vikram Ambalal Sarabhai was born on August 12, 1919, in Ahmedabad, Gujarat, India, into a prominent and wealthy family. His father, Ambalal Sarabhai, was an industrialist and a key figure in the Indian independence movement. His mother, Sarla Devi, was a deeply spiritual and influential figure in his life.

Educational Background

Vikram Sarabhai showed early promise in academics and attended Gujarat College in Ahmedabad for his undergraduate studies. He later moved to Cambridge University in the United Kingdom, where he earned a Tripos in Natural Sciences. Subsequently, he pursued a doctorate at Cambridge in 1947.

Return to India

After completing his education abroad, Sarabhai returned to India with a vision of using science and technology for the country's development. He was deeply influenced by the idea that science could play a crucial role in addressing societal challenges.

Founding INCOSPAR

In 1962, Vikram Sarabhai founded the Indian National Committee for Space Research (INCOSPAR), which later evolved into the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). This marked the beginning of India's space program. Sarabhai was appointed as the chairman of INCOSPAR, and he initiated efforts to harness space technology for national development.

Thumba Equatorial Rocket Launching Station (TERLS)

Under Sarabhai's leadership, India established the Thumba Equatorial Rocket Launching Station (TERLS) in Thiruvananthapuram in 1962. This facility became the focal point for India's early experiments with sounding rockets and space research.

Space Applications and Satellite Launch Vehicles

Sarabhai envisioned the practical applications of space technology for societal benefits. His efforts led to the establishment of the Indian National Satellite System (INSAT), which transformed communication, broadcasting, meteorology, and disaster management in India. He also initiated the development of indigenous satellite launch vehicles.

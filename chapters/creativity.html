Creativity

-edward de bono

about the author

Edward Charles Francis Publius de Bono, a Maltese physician, psychologist, author, inventor, philosopher, and consultant invented the concept of lateral thinking. He is a world-renowned writer and philosopher and the leading authority in the field of creative thinking and the direct teaching of thinking as a skill. With more than 60 books, in 40 languages to his credit, he has people now teaching his methods worldwide. He dedicated his life to inspiring, encouraging and enabling better and more creative thinkers. He created "The Six Thinking Hats', method to enable individuals and teams to be more receptive to new ideas and to develop them constructively. This current lesson is an introduction to his book 'How to have Creative Ideas?'

Creativity as a Talent

Creativity is often regarded as a talent, an innate ability possessed by some individuals. However, it's important to recognize that talent alone does not guarantee creative output. While some people may have a natural inclination towards creative thinking, everyone has the potential to nurture and develop their creative abilities through deliberate practice and exploration.

Uninhibited Creativity

Uninhibited creativity refers to the freedom to think and create without constraints or self-imposed limitations. True creativity often flourishes when individuals allow their minds to wander, explore unconventional ideas, and break away from established norms. Removing inhibitions encourages a more open and expansive approach to problem-solving and idea generation.

Creativity as a Skill

Contrary to the belief that creativity is solely an inherent talent, Edward de Bono emphasizes that creativity is a skill that can be cultivated and honed. Like any skill, it requires practice, intentional effort, and the willingness to embrace different thinking methodologies. By viewing creativity as a skill, individuals are empowered to actively develop and enhance their creative abilities over time.

Creativity is Not a Mystery

Edward de Bono asserts that creativity is not a mysterious, unpredictable force. Instead, he encourages a systematic approach to creative thinking through various techniques such as lateral thinking, the Six Thinking Hats, and other structured methods. By understanding and applying these methods, individuals can demystify the creative process and consistently generate innovative ideas.

The Word Creativity

The term "creativity" is derived from the Latin word "creare," which means "to make" or "to produce." It encompasses the ability to bring something new into existence, whether it be an idea, a work of art, or a solution to a problem. Understanding the etymology of the word provides insight into the fundamental nature of creativity as a generative and transformative force.

Habits of Mind

Cultivating creativity involves developing certain habits of mind. These may include being open-minded, embracing ambiguity, staying curious, and being willing to take risks. By incorporating these habits into daily thinking patterns, individuals create a conducive environment for creative ideas to flourish.

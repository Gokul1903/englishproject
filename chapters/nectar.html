Those who have lost the Nectar

-o.n.v kurup

about the author

Ottaplakkal Neelakandan Velu Kurup (1931-2016), popularly known as O.N.V. Kurup, or O.N.V., is a renowned Malayalam poet. A prolific writer, ONV has a large number of works to his credit poetry as well as prose. Being a lyricist, he has also contributed immensely to the Malayalam film and drama songs. His songs perfected in association with all major music directors are an all-time hit. O.N.V has won awards such as Jnanpith Award, Padma Shri and Padma Bhushan.This poem is translated from the Malayalam by S. Velayudhan and published in The Ancient Lyreby Sahitya Akademi, New Delhi in 2005

poem

It has rained my son,
the courtyard has turned into a stream
and your joy, into paper boats.
You launch them one by one. delighted
as you look at them intently.
As you sit like a legendary god
leading a fleet of ships to far off lands
in good weather
your father sits behind you
and enjoys the sight more.
Your ancestors
you haven't known, see it from afar.
Like their tears of joy, fall
the rain drops here and there.
The droplets of fresh rain
draw circles like flowers in the water...
In an instant, son,
your face is shadowed:
Will it rain again,
will the boats sink?
We are those who have lost
the nectar of pure joy; we grieve over
things that might or might not come to pass.

Universal Experience of Loss

The poem explores the theme of loss, emphasizing that it is a shared experience that transcends individual circumstances or backgrounds. Loss is portrayed as a universal aspect of the human condition that touches everyone at some point in their lives.

Emotional Turmoil and Reflections

Through vivid imagery and metaphorical language, Kurup conveys the emotional turmoil and deep reflections that accompany loss. The poem delves into the complex range of feelings that arise when individuals experience loss, including sadness, longing, and introspection.

Transient Nature of Life

Kurup highlights the transient nature of life, suggesting that loss serves as a reminder of the impermanence of human existence. The poem prompts readers to contemplate the fleeting nature of happiness, beauty, and fulfillment, which are symbolized by the metaphorical "nectar."

Resilience and Search for Meaning

Despite the pain of loss, the poem explores themes of resilience and the human capacity to endure hardship. It suggests that loss can lead individuals on a journey of self-discovery and spiritual growth as they search for meaning amidst adversity.

Shared Humanity

"Those Who Have Lost the Nectar" underscores the shared experiences of humanity, inviting readers to empathize with the struggles and challenges faced by others. The poem fosters a sense of connection and solidarity as individuals navigate the complexities of loss together.
